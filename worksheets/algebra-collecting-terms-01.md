**Tips for practice:**
- Only like terms can be combined (e.g., 2a and 3a are like terms, but 2a and 3b are not)
- Like terms have exactly the same variable part (e.g., x and x are like, but x and x² are not)
- When combining, add or subtract the coefficients (numbers in front) and keep the variable part the same
- Remember: a = 1a, so when you see just 'a' by itself, it means 1a
- For products like ab and ba, they are the same thing (order doesn't matter for multiplication)
